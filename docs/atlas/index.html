E3 Atlas

Evidence, Error, and Explanation

The E3 Atlas is a small concept map for the portfolio. It connects the ideas that appear across projects, Shiny apps, Crosswalk posts and the Bioinformatics Pitfalls Lab.

Projects show what I can build.
Field Notes show how I think.
The Pitfalls Lab shows my judgment.
The Atlas shows how the ideas connect.

How to read the Atlas

Each concept has two definitions:

  • a plain-language definition for quick understanding
  • a technical definition for more precise interpretation

The links are intentionally lightweight. A concept can point to related projects, Crosswalk posts or Pitfalls Lab modules without needing a separate page yet.

Main concept groups

Statistics and interpretation

These concepts support the genetic association Shiny app and Crosswalk posts about uncertainty, evidence and communication.

Examples include odds ratio, confidence interval, statistical significance, effect size, sample size and uncertainty.

Bioinformatics and RNA-seq

These concepts support the RNA-seq pipeline demo and explain how raw sequencing data becomes structured output.

Examples include quality control, read trimming, alignment, quantification, normalization and differential expression.

Pitfalls and data integrity

These concepts connect directly to the Bioinformatics Pitfalls Lab.

Examples include genome build, annotation file, gene ID, batch effect, metadata, low counts, multiple testing and overinterpretation.

Future upgrades

Version 1 is intentionally simple. Later, this Atlas could grow into:

  • a searchable concept table
  • tag filters by topic or project
  • individual concept pages
  • a network map showing connections between concepts
  • backlinks from projects, Crosswalk posts and Pitfalls Lab modules
